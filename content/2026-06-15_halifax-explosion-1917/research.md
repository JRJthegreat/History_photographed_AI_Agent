# Topic: the Halifax explosion 1917

## One-line hook candidate
December 6, 1917. A telegraph operator in Halifax sends his last message at 8:55 AM — then dies at his post to save 300 strangers on a train.

## Verified facts

### Who
- **Patrick Vincent "Vince" Coleman** — Canadian Government Railways train dispatcher, Richmond railyard, Halifax
- **SS Mont-Blanc** — French cargo ship carrying munitions
- **SS Imo** — Norwegian relief vessel
- The Mi'kmaq community at Tufts Cove (entirely wiped out)

### When
- **Collision:** 8:45 AM, December 6, 1917
- **Coleman's final telegram:** moments before 9:04 AM
- **Explosion:** 9:04:35 AM, December 6, 1917

### Where
- Halifax Harbour, Nova Scotia, Canada
- Coleman's telegraph post was ~250 yards (≈230 m) from Pier 6

### What happened
1. Mont-Blanc (2,925 metric tons of explosives: TNT, picric acid, benzol, guncotton) and Imo collide in the Narrows of Halifax Harbour at 8:45 AM.
2. The collision damages benzol barrels on Mont-Blanc's deck; vapours ignite from spark. Crew abandons ship, doesn't warn the city.
3. Citizens of Halifax gather at windows to watch the burning ship — an estimated thousands of spectators.
4. Vince Coleman learns of the danger from a sailor. He begins fleeing with coworker William Lovett, then turns back alone to his telegraph key.
5. Coleman sends a series of telegrams to halt incoming trains. His final, verbatim:
   > **"Hold up the train. Ammunition ship afire in harbor making for Pier 6 and will explode. Guess this will be my last message. Good-bye boys."**
6. At 9:04:35 AM, Mont-Blanc detonates. Largest man-made explosion in history until Hiroshima (1945).
7. Coleman dies at his post.

### The reversal
He came back. He'd already gotten out. He turned around to send a telegram to people he would never meet.

### Human cost
- **1,782 dead** (Halifax + Dartmouth + Tufts Cove)
- **~9,000 injured**
- **249 surgical eye removals** performed in the days following — civilians who had been watching the fire through windows
- **16 people lost both eyes**
- **1,600+ buildings destroyed**
- The Saint John overnight train was halted in time. **~300 passengers survived because Coleman's telegram reached the dispatcher.**

### The aftermath nobody talks about
A **tsunami rose 18 metres (59 feet)** above the high-water mark on the Halifax side, sweeping the Imo onto the Dartmouth shore and erasing the entire Mi'kmaq settlement at Tufts Cove — a community that had lived there for generations.

## Risks / unknowns
- No verified photograph of Vince Coleman himself in Wikimedia (Maritime Museum may have one, separate sourcing). Reel may need to anchor visually on the telegraph station, the harbour, or the aftermath rather than the man's face.
- Specific text of his earlier (non-final) telegrams is not verified — only the final "Good-bye boys" message is verbatim-sourced.
- The "watching the fire through windows" detail is mechanistically established (eye injury pattern) but the count of spectators is an estimate, not a precise figure.

## Recommended Reel anchor
Lead with **Coleman's reversal**: he was leaving. He came back to die at a telegraph key for people he would never meet. The visuals are the aftermath he never saw.
